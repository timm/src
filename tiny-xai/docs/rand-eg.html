Reproducible randomness

Later demos shuffle and sample, so first: rand, a seeded 16807 Lehmer generator that repeats exactly on sbcl and clisp (Common Lisp’s own random does not). The second check sums three uniforms 10,000 times (Irwin-Hall): mean lands on 0, sd on 1 – testing rand, add, and Welford in one shot.

call returns what
(rand n) float 0..n next seeded random
(rint n) int 0..n-1 random integer
(add i v) v Welford-update num i

Seeded rand repeats; 10k Irwin-Hall -> mean 0, sd 1

    

    

      
(defun eg--rand (&aux a b (i (make-num)))
  (setf *seed* 1 a (rand)
        *seed* 1 b (rand))
  (format t "~&rand ~,3f ~,3f rint ~a~%" a b (rint 10))
  (assert (= a b))
  (assert (< 0 a 1))
  (dotimes (k 10000)
    (add i (/ (- (+ (rand) (rand) (rand)) 1.5) 0.5)))
  (format t "~&irwin-hall mu ~,3f sd ~,3f~%"
          (mid i) (spread i))
  (assert (< (abs (mid i)) 0.05))
  (assert (< (abs (- (spread i) 1)) 0.05)))
